F.A.Q.'s

Frequently Asked Questions

Overview

At it's heart it is a list of questions followed up by answers. The frequently asked part is often the result of creative license. But basically it is a common convention for either dealing with truly frequently asked questions, or an avenue for an elevator pitch for either the site or a part of that site.

Candidates

  • A <dl>
  • A <details> and <summary>
  • A <ul>
  • A <ol>
  • A heading element and content

Wow, that's a monster list of candidates.

Let's get this out in the open first. Everyone and their mother uses <dl> for F.A.Q.'s. It's just how it's done. Every public answer to this question will point you to using a <dl>. We certainly won't argue that isn't true. Some people have argued against the sematics of a definition list not fitting this usage. That's because in HTML 4 a <dl> was a definition list. However in HTML5 things have changed, the <dl> is a description list. So I think that puts you specifically in the clear semantically.

So, is there any reason to discuss the rest? Sure. I think that <dl> is the best choice in general, but there are some cases where you might want to go with a different answer.

The other list tags can be useful, and might be appropriate in some cases. The big stumbling block there is that for both the <ul> and <ol> each node in a <li> is one stand alone piece of content. If you want to mark up a question and an answer, you have to have include multiples of <div>, <span> or <p> in some sort of awkward combo.

A <dl> can be great for short answers to many short questions. However, when you have long answers or few questions, a heading with content below it might work better for you.

The elements <details> and <summary> are new in HTML5. They instruct the browser to hide the main content in the <details> element, and display the content in <summary> as a clickable toggle to show the content in the <details> element. It's pretty cool and baked right into the browser. However as of this time, it's only available on Chrome.

Verdict

Please be like every other website author on the planet and for the love of goodness use a <dl>. Okay, you got really long winded answers, go with a heading and content.
