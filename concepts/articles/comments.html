Comments

A list of audience provided responses to a piece of content.

This entry refers a list of comments on a blog. For individual comments that make up this list see comment

Overview

Comments are basically just big lists of individual comments.

Candidates

  • <div>
  • <dl>
  • <ol>
  • <section>
  • <ul>

At first glace, one of the lists makes a good candidate. This is a big honkin' list of comments. Semantically, the <ol> makes a strong showing as comments are usally listed in chronilogical order, and therefore haivng the numbers part of the display of the items is pretty good. However it's hard to style the <ol> numbers independent of the text. It can be done, but involves adding more elements to the content. The <ul> is a better choice here, you can just turn off the bullets and style away. The <dl> might be semantically correct, but seems like you're just showing off your use of obscure elements. Not that there is anything wrong with that.

You could go with a <div>. The w3c discourages using a <div> when there is a more specific element to use, and in this case you have several.

A <section> refers to a group of related bits of content. A list of comments in a blog definately fit the bill.

Verdict

We recomend the <section>. The <ul> is a good backup. However a <ul> requires a bit of extra CSS editing, that's unnecessary if you start with the plainer <section>.
