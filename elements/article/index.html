Trying to answer that age old question:
Is that a div, a span, or something else.

<article>

A standalone piece of content

Overview

If you are using the words "entry," "post," or obviously "article" to refer to the content in other places, <article> is for you.

An <article> is meant to denote any pice of content that could stand on it's own. The most perfect example of this is a blog post. A blog post although part of a larger site is a single consumable chunk, and does not require any of the rest of the content on the site to be something that one would be interested in. If you are using the words "entry," "post," or obviously "article" to refer to the content in other places, <article> is for you.

However you are not restricted to those uses. "Standalone" can mean a fair number of things. For example are comments appropriate for <article>? At first glace no, but if you allow direct linking to them via anchors, then it can be reasoned that you could count a comment as standalone and therefore appropriate to deal with as an <article>.

Conflicting or Confusing Elements

<div>

Anything that can be referred to by <article> can work as a <div>. In this case <div> is less specific then <article>.

<section>

These two tags were both added in HTML5.
