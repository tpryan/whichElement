<nav>

A list of commands usually as part of a web application, as opposed to web site.

Overview

The <nav> is intended to provide a list of links that lead to other anchors on the page, or pages within the site. It's for navigation. Remember before HTML5? You wanted to have site navigation, and you gave it an id of nav? It's just like that.

Conflicting or Confusing Elements

<menu>

The easiest way to keep these two clear in your head is:

  • The <nav> element is for navigating through links on your site.
  • The <menu> element for giving commands like you would ordering off of a menu at a restaurant… That's terrible. You know what? Just remember the one for <nav>
