One of the main challenges we see in building semantic content is picking what tag to use when. This site seeks to help with that. Now, lest we seem all judgy and preachy let me get a few beliefs out there:

  • A perfectly semantic site does not exist.
  • Even if it did, someone would disagree with its semantics.
  • Therefore semantics for semantic's sake is pointless.

However, semantics are about more then just semantics for semantic's sake. Semantics can be about accessibility, SEO, or just good developer to developer communication of intent. They can be used to created better hooks for CSS and DOM manipulation.

Therefore we approach this site with the following in mind:

  • Sematics exist along a continuum, something can be more or less semantic
  • We encourage semantics, but not at the cost of reasonableless.

Our goal is to suggest things to help you make a good decision about what you should do to be semantic. We certainly don't think we have any authority to command you to do things to be absolutely semantic.
